The Foundation

Built for
Two Missions.

The Eco Yurt village does something rare in the nonprofit world: it serves the mission and funds it at the same time.

Six to eight custom-designed eco yurts sit at the edge of the Sylvan Hills campus ‚Äî built from sustainably sourced timber and natural materials chosen to blend into the Blue Ridge forest canopy. No hard edges, no synthetic surfaces. Every structure is designed to feel like it grew out of the land rather than being placed on it.

During the three Freedom Summit windows ‚Äî and every Coaching Hope residential weekend ‚Äî the yurts are home base for the cohorts driving the program. For the other nine months, they open to the public as a destination short-term rental. The mountain pays for itself. That revenue flows directly into the programs it housed.

Why the Blue Ridge STR Market Works
Compressed Supply McDowell County has limited quality short-term rental inventory. Unique, design-forward eco lodging anchored in place and purpose commands premium rates with less competition.
Year-Round Demand Fall foliage (Sept‚ÄìNov) and summer outdoor recreation (Jun‚ÄìAug) create two peak demand windows that align almost perfectly with the non-summit months. Winter is soft but manageable with the right marketing; spring builds fast.
Sustainability as Premium Guests aren't just renting a pod. They're staying in a sustainably built structure on a working athletic campus, walking trails used by summit athletes, and contributing to a community land trust. That narrative commands price premiums on Hipcamp, direct booking, and sustainability-focused travel platforms.
